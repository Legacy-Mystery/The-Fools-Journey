Foundational Tarot

Section 1: The Architecture of the 78 Cards

The Tarot deck is a unified system of seventy-eight symbolic cards. It functions as both a cosmic map and a psychological mirror, showing the journey of the soul from innocence to enlightenment, and reflecting the day-to-day influences on that path.

The deck is divided into three distinct groups, or Arcanas (Latin for “secrets”):

1. The Major Arcana (22 Cards, 0–21)

  • The Great Secrets: These cards represent major life lessons, spiritual truths, and powerful, inescapable archetypal forces. When a Major Arcana card appears in a reading, it signifies that a powerful, life-altering energy is at play.
  • The Fool’s Journey: The Major Arcana tells a story—the quest of The Fool (0). He begins with innocence and travels through every human experience (love, hardship, success, death) until he achieves completion with The World (21).

2. The Minor Arcana (56 Cards)

  • The Lesser Secrets: : These cards deal with the practical, daily ebb and flow of life—our immediate concerns, temporary conflicts, and small victories. They describe how the Major Arcana energies are playing out on the ground level.
  • The Four Suits / Elements: The Minor Arcana is broken down into four suits, each corresponding to an element of nature and a facet of human experience:
    • Wands (Element of Fire 🔥): Domain is Will, Career, Identity. Keywords include Passion, energy, growth, action, enterprise, ambition.
    • Cups (Element of Water 💧): Domain is Emotion, Relationships, Soul. Keywords include Love, feelings, intuition, creativity, dreams, spiritual connection.
    • Swords (Element of Air 💨): Domain is Intellect, Thought, Conflict. Keywords include Logic, truth, challenge, hardship, communication, justice.
    • Pentacles (Element of Earth 🌍): Domain is Material World, Body, Wealth. Keywords include Money, home, security, health, manifestation, resources.

3. The Court Cards (16 Cards)

  • These are the personalities of the Minor Arcana (Page, Knight, Queen, King). They often represent actual people in the querent’s life, but can also represent manners of behavior or attitudes that the querent should adopt.
  • The Hierarchy: :
    • Pages: The Student or Messenger. Represent new ideas, beginning an endeavor, or youthful energy.
    • Knights: The Action-TakerorMovement. Represent rapid change, physical journey, or an impulsive approach.
    • Queens: The Feeler or Master of Emotion. Represents mature, internal, feminine power, focused on nurturing and intuition.
    • Kings: The Ruler or Master of the Domain. Represent mature, external, masculine power, focused on structure, control, and achievement.

Section 3: The Numerology of the Minor Arcana

  • Ace (1) - Principle: Unity / Potential:The seed of the element. A pure blast of energy, a gift, a new beginning.
  • Two (2) - Principle: Balance / Choice: Duality, cooperation, decision-making, partnership, waiting.
  • Three (3) - Principle: Growth / Synthesis: The first tangible result. Collaboration, expansion, celebration, fertility.
  • Four (4) - Principle: Stability / Stagnation: Foundation, structure, rest, security, but can lead to being stuck or rigid.
  • Five (5) - Principle: Conflict / Loss: Disruption, strife, challenge, testing limits, moving out of comfort.
  • Six (6) - Principle: Harmony / Shift: Resolution after the five. Movement, connection, giving and receiving, victory.
  • Seven (7) - Principle: Triumph / Illusion: Persistence, hidden obstacles, testing faith, personal integrity, illusion.
  • Eight (8) - Principle: Action / Movement: Rapid progress, power through organization, putting effort into motion, mastery.
  • Nine (9) - Principle: Fulfillment / Climax: Nearing the end, wish-fulfillment, great satisfaction, but also anxiety about loss.
  • Ten (10) - Principle: Completion / Burden: The end of a cycle. Carrying a load, finality, conclusion, new cycle beginning.

Section 5: Essential Tarot Spreads for Clarity

  1. The Three-Card Quick Read (The Foundation)
    • Purpose: Simple, direct insight into a situation’s timeline or dynamic.
    • Layout: Three cards laid in a horizontal line.
      • Position 1 (Root): The cause, the past, the underlying issue.
      • Position 2 (Trunk): The present action, the immediate influence, what is happening now.
      • Position 3 (Flower): The likely future outcome if the present course continues.
  2. The Simple Decision Spread (The Fork in the Road)
    • Purpose: To gain insight before making a binary choice between two paths.
    • Layout: Three cards: A, B, and C (the central card).
      • Position A (Path of Option 1): What happens if I choose this path?
      • Position B (Path of Option 2): What happens if I choose this path?
      • Position C (Guidance): What is the overall advice for making this choice? (Drawn last).
  3. The Celtic Cross Spread (The Deep Dive)
    • Purpose: The most comprehensive spread for analyzing a complex situation, its influences, and long-term trajectory.
    • Layout: Ten cards forming a cross and a staff.
      • Positions 1 & 2 (The Cross): Present Situation (1) and Obstacle/Influence (2). Card 2 crosses Card 1, showing what is working against the central issue.
      • Position 3 (Root of the Matter): Subconscious origin or past event behind the issue.
      • Position 4 (Fading Past): A recent influence now passing away.
      • Position 5 (Goal/Crown): Conscious desires or best possible outcome.
      • Position 6 (Near Future): What is approaching in the coming weeks or months.
      • Position 7 (The Self): Your attitude, feelings, and role in the situation.
      • Position 8 (Environment): How others see you, or the people/world affecting the situation.
      • Position 9 (Hopes/Fears): What you secretly want, or what you worry will happen.
      • Position 10 (The Outcome): The ultimate destination if all influences play out as predicted.

Section 6: The Ethics and Maintenance of the Deck

To maintain a healthy magical relationship with your deck, a code of ethics and practice must be followed.

  1. The Cardinal Rules for Reading
    1. Do Not Predict Death (or Literal Disaster): The Tarot reveals probabilities based on current energy, not absolute fates. Refuse any question that asks for a fixed, negative outcome, especially regarding death or illness. You are a guide, not a doomsayer.
    2. Empowerment, Not Dependence: The goal of a reading is to show the querent their agency. Frame the outcome not as a final truth, but as a guidepost they can change by altering their actions.
    3. Respect Free Will: Never read for a third party (e.g., “Will my neighbor get divorced?”) unless the query is strictly about the querent’s relationship to that person. Their will and life path are not yours to inspect.
  2. Cleansing and Protection
    • Initial Cleansing: When you first acquire a deck, cleanse it of all previous energies. Methods include:
      • Knocking: Giving the deck three sharp knocks to “wake it up” and clear it.
      • Moonlight: Leaving the deck under the full moon overnight.
      • Smoke Cleansing: Passing the deck through the smoke of sage, cedar, or incense.
    • Resting Place: Store the deck in a protective container (such as a silk cloth or wooden box) to shield it from stray or distracting energies.
  3. The Reader’s Log
    • Log Entry Requirements:
      1. Date and Time: Note the lunar phase or time of day.
      2. Question: Record the exact question asked.
      3. Spread Used: (e.g., 3‑Card: Past / Present / Future).
      4. Cards Drawn: List the cards and the positions they landed in. Note whether they were Upright (U) or Reversed (R).
      5. Interpretation: Your immediate analysis before the outcome is known.
      6. The Outcome (Follow‑Up): After a week or a month, review how the reading played out and record what you learned.
